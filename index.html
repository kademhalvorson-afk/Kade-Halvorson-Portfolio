About Me

Design is how complex information becomes usable. Through experience in video production, digital publishing, and presentation development, I’ve built a skill set focused on turning ideas, research, and content into visuals that are clear, engaging, and easy to act on.

At the University of Minnesota, I worked as a Video Operations Intern, where I strengthened my skills in video editing. That experience taught me how to shape content with intention and create polished outputs that hold attention. As a Digital Publications Intern with the American Academy of Neurology, I focused on graphic design, infographics, and presentation development, creating branded materials that strengthened professional communications and made complex information more visually engaging and accessible.

In my current role at Child Trends, I create presentations that help translate research and strategic content into compelling visual stories for a range of audiences. From structuring slides for clarity to designing layouts that make dense material feel approachable, I focus on work that is thoughtful, polished, and effective.

Based in Minnesota. Available for part-time and contract engagements.
